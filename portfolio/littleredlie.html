Little Red Lie

An adventure game about debt, family and the truth about honesty.

About the Game

Little Red Lie is a linear interactive fiction experience. The game is available via Steam, Humble and itch.io for Windows and Mac. Mobile versions are also available for iOS and Android.

The game is between six and eight hours in length, with a novel-length script of approximately eighty-thousand words. It was built in Unity 5 with the Adventure Creator extension.

I developed this game personally and released it via my own studio, WZO Games Inc.

Dishonesty as Mechanic

In addition to individual player interactions being contextualized as dishonest, the game also utilizes red text in the dialogue of the player character to denote something said in the spirit of dishonesty - this creates an intriguing layer of subtext that sustains the considerable amount of reading involved in the game.

Lying is also deeply connected to the plot, themes and social commentary within the game itself - I undertook great effort to create a very coherent and thought-provoking look at deception, and the role that it plays in our lives and relationships.

Creative Direction & Visuals

In addition to writing and development, I was also fully responsible for the creative direction of three pixel artists and one CG artist. I designed the tilemaps and UI personally.

While possessing no fantastical elements, I took very conscious inspirations from the survival horror genre, frequently utilizing it to augment the storytelling and narrative in a way that matched the heaviness of the words with a dread-filled atmosphere.

Music & SFX

Utilizing MIDI melodies sourced from Loopmasters, I arranged all of the music in the game via Logic Studio. Sound FX were sourced from a variety of CC0 assets on freesound.org and tweaked appropriately to create a heavy, synth-based vibe.

Press & Reception

Little Red Lie has gathered a wide variety of positive impressions from fans and critics alike. The game has >90% positive Steam reviews, with many players writing multiple paragraphs praising the writing and narrative.
